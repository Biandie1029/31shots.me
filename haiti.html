Introduction

In the early 1700s, Spain agreed to share a portion of the island Hispaniola with the French. This portion of the island, now known as Haiti, became one of the most profitable parts of the Carribean at the time. However this financial success was made possible only by the labor of Africans who were enslaved on the island. Nearly 200 years later, a man by the name of Toussaint L'Ouverture led those slaves in a revolution that resulted in Haiti becoming the first independent black republic in the world.

Fun Fact: The name Haiti (or Repiblik de Ayiti) meaning "Land of the High Mountains" was originally the name of the entire island of Hispaniola.

The Constitution

The Constitution of Haiti was established on March 10th, 1987 underwent major revisions in June 2012. The document is currently referred to as "The ammended 1987 constitution".

Military and Security

Security Forces

Haiti disbanded its original armed forces in 1995 and officially established an Army in command in 2018. It also has a Ministry of Justice and Public Security called the Haitian National Police, or PNH

Haitian National Police

The Haitian National Police has two units: The Presidential Guard and a paramilitary rapid-response Motorized Intervention unit. It is responsible for a variety of security needs in Haiti, including:

  • Police
  • Corrections
  • Fire Response
  • Airport Security
  • Port Security
  • Coast Guard Functions

Personnel Strengths

Haiti currently has 2,000 military troops with goal of 5,000. The National Police has anywhere from 9,000 to 13,0000 officers.
